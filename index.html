Frequently asked

Questions clients ask first.

If your question isn't answered here, write to us — we reply the same business day.

Who do you act for?

Companies, individuals, and international law firms that need Egyptian counsel. We take instructions directly from clients, and we also act as Cairo correspondent for firms based abroad.

What areas of law do you cover?

Litigation (criminal, civil, commercial and sports), arbitration and award enforcement, debt collection, corporate and commercial advisory, bilingual contract review, and regulatory compliance — all under Egyptian law.

Who leads the work?

Founding partner Mohamed Elasuty and the firm's partners lead matters personally, supported by the firm's lawyers. Dedicated partners head the debt-collection and enforcement practice and the corporate desk.

What languages do you work in?

Arabic and English, across every matter — from court filings and contracts to correspondence with international clients and counsel.

How are fees structured?

A fixed fee for defined-scope work, or an agreed hourly basis, set out in writing before we begin. You will not receive an open-ended bill.
