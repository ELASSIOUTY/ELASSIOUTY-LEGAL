How we work

Three decades of practice, structured for international clients.

What the four principles below mean in practice, on the matters the practice accepts.

01

Partner on every matter

Mohamed Elasuty scopes work personally and signs off the engagement. There is no staffing hand-off after the engagement letter is signed.

02

Counsel-led, bench-augmented

The lead partner leads. An Arabic-native bench supports defined-term drafting, disclosure logs and court filings where it adds depth — never as a substitute for senior judgement.

03

Confidentiality that survives outside-counsel review

Encrypted transfer, least-privilege access, segregated review environments and clean-room handling of marked material. NDAs and outside-counsel security schedules signed before any document leaves your firm.

04

Drafted, not calibrated

Common-law and Sharia-aware drafting read as a single discipline. We learn your firm's house style on the first matter and hold to it.

Where the work sits

Enforcement — debt collection, civil, criminal and sports matters — runs through Egyptian courts. We supervise that process end-to-end. Advisory work — contract review and translation supervision — is delivered remotely to GCC and UK firms.

Frequently asked

Questions partners usually ask first.

If the answer to your question isn't here, write to us — we reply the same business day.

What jurisdictions do you cover?

We are qualified in Egypt and act only under Egyptian law. Court-based work — debt collection, civil, criminal and sports matters — is conducted before the Egyptian courts. Advisory work is delivered remotely to instructing firms in the GCC and the United Kingdom. We act as your Cairo correspondent; we are not qualified in, and do not advise on, English or GCC law.

Who supervises the work?

Mohamed Elasuty, Managing Partner, scopes and signs off every matter personally. An Arabic-native bench supports drafting, disclosure logs and filings, but senior counsel leads throughout — there is no hand-off to junior staff on substantive work.

How do you handle confidentiality and privilege?

Documents move by encrypted transfer into a segregated, least-privilege review environment. NDAs and outside-counsel security schedules are signed before any material leaves your firm, and marked material is handled clean-room. We work to the confidentiality standards your own clients expect.

What are typical turnaround times?

We acknowledge every new matter the same business day with scope, fee and the supervising counsel. Substantive turnaround depends on the matter — a contract review runs to a different clock than contested litigation — and we commit to a defined timeline in the engagement letter.

How is pricing structured?

Either a fixed fee for defined-scope work or a defined hourly cap, agreed before we begin. You will not receive an open-ended bill; the fee basis is set in writing at the outset.
